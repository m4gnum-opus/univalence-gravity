## 6. 3D Edge Curvature

In 3 dimensions, the combinatorial curvature lives on **edges** (not vertices). At an interior edge e with valence v:

> κ(e) = 2π − v · (π/2)

For the Honeycomb-145 patch, all 12 central-cube edges are fully surrounded by 5 cubes:

| Edge class | Valence | Count | κ₂₀ (twentieths) | Interpretation |
|------------|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| ev5 | 5 | 12 | −5 | Hyperbolic (full {4,3,5} condition) |

**Total edge curvature:**

```
12 · (−5) = −60  (in twentieths)
```

Verified by `refl` in `Bulk/Honeycomb145Curvature.agda`:

```agda

totalH145Curvature-check = refl
```

The total is `negsuc 59` because −60 in ℤ is `negsuc 59` (representing −(59+1) = −60). This is identical to the curvature of Dense-50, Dense-100, Dense-200, and Dense-1000 — all Dense patches have the same central-cube edge curvature because all 12 edges are fully surrounded at every scale.

## 11. What the Honeycomb-145 Patch Does NOT Exercise

- **Area law / Bekenstein–Hawking bound.** The discrete area law S ≤ area and the half-bound 2·S ≤ area are not formalized for Honeycomb-145. These are verified on Dense-100 ([`instances/dense-100.md`](dense-100.md)), Dense-200 ([`instances/dense-200.md`](dense-200.md)), and Dense-1000 ([`instances/dense-1000.md`](dense-1000.md)).

- **Resolution tower membership.** The Honeycomb-145 is registered as a standalone `TowerLevel` in `Bridge/SchematicTower.agda` but does not participate in the primary 3-level Dense resolution tower (Dense-50 → Dense-100 → Dense-200). It serves as an additional verified instance demonstrating spectrum growth.

- **Subadditivity / monotonicity structural properties.** These are exercised on the 6-tile star patch ([`instances/star-patch.md`](star-patch.md)) and the 11-tile filled patch ([`instances/filled-patch.md`](filled-patch.md)).

- **Parameterized step invariance / dynamics.** The dynamics layer operates on the 6-tile star with parameterized weights. See [`instances/star-patch.md`](star-patch.md).

- **Gauge connections / quantum superposition.** The gauge and quantum layers are exercised on the 6-tile star. See [`instances/star-patch.md`](star-patch.md).

- **Curvature-agnostic bridge (Wick rotation).** The Wick rotation is demonstrated between AdS ({5,4}) and dS ({5,3}) on the star patch. See [`instances/desitter-patch.md`](desitter-patch.md).

- **2D polygon-complex / vertex curvature.** The Honeycomb-145 is 3D with edge curvature; the 2D vertex curvature and Gauss–Bonnet theorem are exercised by the filled patch ([`instances/filled-patch.md`](filled-patch.md)).

The Honeycomb-145 patch provides a concrete data point localizing the spectrum growth from maxS=8 to maxS=9:

| Dense-100 | 100 | 8 | — |

The growth from 8 to 9 occurs somewhere between 100 and 145 cells. After 145 cells, the maxS value stabilizes at 9 across all tested patch sizes (up to 1000 cells with `max_region_cells=5`; the Dense-1000 achieves 9 at `max_region_cells=6`).

This means:
- The Dense-200 and Dense-1000 patches **confirm** the maxS=9 value already achieved by Honeycomb-145.
- The monotonicity witness from Dense-100 to Honeycomb-145 is `(1, refl)` (strict growth).
- The monotonicity witness from Honeycomb-145 to Dense-200 is `(0, refl)` (flat — spectrum already saturated at 9).
