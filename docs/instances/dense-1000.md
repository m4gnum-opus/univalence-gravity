# Dense-1000 Instance

**The 1000-cell {4,3,5} Dense patch — the largest formally verified 3D holographic bridge instance, with adaptive region enumeration and three levels of entropy-area constraint.**

**Modules:** `Common/Dense1000Spec.agda` (AUTO-GEN), `Boundary/Dense1000Cut.agda` (AUTO-GEN), `Bulk/Dense1000Chain.agda`, `Bulk/Dense1000Curvature.agda`, `Bridge/Dense1000Obs.agda`, `Boundary/Dense1000AreaLaw.agda` (AUTO-GEN), `Boundary/Dense1000HalfBound.agda` (AUTO-GEN)

**Role:** The Dense-1000 patch extends the Dense resolution tower to a fourth level, demonstrating that the orbit reduction architecture scales to 10,000+ regions with no increase in proof complexity. It carries the same **three levels of entropy-area constraint** as Dense-100 and Dense-200 — the RT correspondence (S = L), the discrete area law (S ≤ area), and the sharp Bekenstein–Hawking half-bound (2·S ≤ area with tight achiever). The key architectural innovation is **adaptive `max_region_cells`**: at 1000 cells, boundary cells are farther from the dense core, so bounded-size regions achieve lower min-cuts than smaller patches; the generator starts at `max_region_cells=6` (instead of the usual 5) to restore monotonicity with Dense-200 (maxS=9). This is the **largest Agda data type in the repository** — `Dense1000Spec.agda` has 10,317 region constructors and a 10,317-clause classification function.

## 3. Adaptive `max_region_cells`

The Dense-1000 patch introduces a key innovation not present in Dense-50 through Dense-200: **adaptive region enumeration size**.

### 3.1 The Problem

At `max_region_cells=5` (the standard value used for Dense-50, Dense-100, and Dense-200), the Dense-1000 patch achieves only maxS=8 — one less than Dense-200's maxS=9. This is because boundary cells in a 1000-cell patch are farther from the dense core than in a 200-cell patch; bounded-size regions of 5 cells can't probe deep enough into the multiply-connected bulk to find the highest min-cut values.

This would break the monotonicity property of the resolution tower: the max min-cut must not decrease from Dense-200 (maxS=9) to Dense-1000. A non-monotone step would mean the holographic depth appears to *shrink* with resolution — physically inconsistent.

### 3.2 The Fix

The generator script `12b_generate_dense1000.py` implements **adaptive `max_region_cells`**: it starts at `max_region_cells=6` and increments if necessary until the target max min-cut (≥9, matching Dense-200) is achieved.

From the entropic convergence analysis (`14c_entropic_convergence_sup_half.py`):

| max_region_cells | maxS at 1000 cells | Achieves target? |
|------------------|---------------------|------------------|
| 5 | 8 | ✗ |
| **6** | **9** | **✓** |
| 7 | 10+ | (not needed) |

At `max_region_cells=6`, the 6-cell boundary subsets can form regions that probe deep enough into the bulk to find min-cut=9 separating surfaces. The generator confirmed monotonicity was restored and stopped at `max_region_cells=6`.

### 3.3 Impact on Region Count

The larger `max_region_cells` produces significantly more regions:

| Patch | max_region_cells | Regions | Orbits | Lines (Spec) |
|-------|-----------------|---------|--------|-------------|
| Dense-200 | 5 | 1,246 | 9 | 1,446 |
| **Dense-1000** | **6** | **10,317** | **9** | **11,379** |

The 10,317-constructor `D1000Region` type is the largest single data type in the repository (11,379 lines for `Dense1000Spec.agda`). Despite this, the orbit count stays at 9 — the same as Dense-200. The orbit reduction factor increases from 138× (Dense-200) to **1146×** (Dense-1000).

The Dense-1000 patch uses the same **orbit reduction architecture** as Dense-100 and Dense-200. The 10,317 regions share only 9 distinct min-cut values. The observable functions `S-cut` and `L-min` are defined on the small orbit type `D1000OrbitRep` (9 constructors), then composed with a classification function `classify1000 : D1000Region → D1000OrbitRep` (10,317 clauses).

> κ(e) = 2π − v · (π/2)

For the Dense-1000 patch, all 12 central-cube edges are fully surrounded by 5 cubes:

## 9. The Resolution Tower Role

The Dense-1000 patch extends the Dense resolution tower to a fourth level:

| Level | Patch | Cells | Regions | Orbits | Max S | max_rc | Monotone |
|-------|-------|-------|---------|--------|-------|--------|----------|
| 0 | Dense-50 | 50 | 139 | — | 7 | 5 | — |
| 1 | Dense-100 | 100 | 717 | 8 | 8 | 5 | (1, refl) |
| 2 | Dense-200 | 200 | 1,246 | 9 | 9 | 5 | (1, refl) |
| **3** | **Dense-1000** | **1000** | **10,317** | **9** | **9** | **6** | **(0, refl)** |

The min-cut spectrum is monotone: 7 → 8 → 9 → 9. The Dense-1000 level has **flat monotonicity** with Dense-200 — both achieve maxS=9, so the witness is `(0, refl)`. This is non-strict but valid: the holographic depth does not decrease.

The flatness at maxS=9 between Dense-200 and Dense-1000 suggests that 9 may be a saturation point for the Dense strategy at `max_region_cells≤6`. Achieving maxS=10 would likely require `max_region_cells=7` or higher, producing correspondingly larger region counts.

## 13. What the Dense-1000 Patch Does NOT Exercise

- **Strict monotonicity beyond Dense-200.** The Dense-1000 has maxS=9, identical to Dense-200 — the tower step is `(0, refl)` (flat). Strict growth would require deeper region probing (`max_region_cells ≥ 7`) or a different growth strategy.

- **Subadditivity / monotonicity structural properties.** These are exercised on the 6-tile star patch ([`instances/star-patch.md`](star-patch.md)) and the 11-tile filled patch ([`instances/filled-patch.md`](filled-patch.md)).

- **Parameterized step invariance / dynamics.** The dynamics layer operates on the 6-tile star with parameterized weights. See [`instances/star-patch.md`](star-patch.md).

- **Gauge connections / quantum superposition.** The gauge and quantum layers are exercised on the 6-tile star. See [`instances/star-patch.md`](star-patch.md).

- **Curvature-agnostic bridge (Wick rotation).** The Wick rotation is demonstrated between AdS ({5,4}) and dS ({5,3}) on the star patch. See [`instances/desitter-patch.md`](desitter-patch.md).

- **2D polygon-complex / vertex curvature.** The Dense-1000 is 3D with edge curvature; the 2D vertex curvature and Gauss–Bonnet theorem are exercised by the filled patch ([`instances/filled-patch.md`](filled-patch.md)).

## 15. Historical Significance

The Dense-1000 patch is the repository's **largest-scale verification target** — the point where the oracle-driven architecture demonstrates it can handle an order of magnitude more regions than Dense-200 without any change to the proof infrastructure.

### 15.1 The Adaptive max_region_cells Innovation

Before Dense-1000, all Dense patches used `max_region_cells=5`. The discovery that boundary cells in larger patches require larger region sizes to probe the same holographic depth is a concrete instance of a general phenomenon in hyperbolic geometry: as the bulk grows, the boundary moves farther from the dense core, and boundary regions must grow to "reach" the same entanglement structure.

The adaptive fix is simple — increase `max_region_cells` from 5 to 6 — but architecturally significant: it demonstrates that the oracle can compensate for geometric effects by adjusting enumeration parameters, while the proof architecture (orbit reduction, generic bridge, `abstract` barrier) remains unchanged.

### 15.2 Scale Testing of the `abstract` Barrier

The `Dense1000AreaLaw.agda` module (29,736 lines) and `Dense1000HalfBound.agda` (19,436 lines) are the largest `abstract`-sealed proof modules in the repository. Together they contain 20,634 `(k, refl)` witnesses sealed behind `abstract`. This validates that Agda's `abstract` keyword scales to modules of this size without degrading type-checking performance for downstream consumers — the barrier prevents re-normalization of the 10,317-case proof regardless of module size.

### 15.3 The Flat Monotonicity Observation

The Dense-1000 achieves maxS=9, identical to Dense-200. This flat monotonicity (witness `(0, refl)`) raises the question: is 9 a fundamental limit, or is it a consequence of the `max_region_cells` cap? The entropic convergence analysis from script 14c suggests the latter: at `max_region_cells=7`, maxS=10 is achievable. The bound S ≤ area/2 continues to hold regardless — the half-bound is independent of the max min-cut value.
