# Dense-100 Instance

**The 100-cell {4,3,5} Dense patch — the first orbit-reduced 3D holographic bridge instance, carrying three levels of entropy-area constraint.**

**Modules:** `Common/Dense100Spec.agda` (AUTO-GEN), `Boundary/Dense100Cut.agda` (AUTO-GEN), `Bulk/Dense100Chain.agda` (AUTO-GEN), `Bulk/Dense100Curvature.agda` (AUTO-GEN), `Bridge/Dense100Obs.agda` (AUTO-GEN), `Boundary/Dense100AreaLaw.agda` (AUTO-GEN, `abstract`), `Boundary/Dense100HalfBound.agda` (AUTO-GEN, `abstract`), `Bridge/Dense100Equiv.agda` (DEAD CODE), `Bridge/Dense100Thermodynamics.agda`

**Role:** The Dense-100 patch is the architectural pivot of the repository. It is the first instance to use the **orbit reduction strategy** (717 regions classified into 8 orbit representatives by min-cut value), reducing proof obligations from 717 to 8 cases via a 1-line lifting. It carries **three levels of entropy-area constraint** — the RT correspondence (S = L), the discrete area law (S ≤ area), and the sharp Bekenstein–Hawking half-bound (2·S ≤ area with tight achiever) — and serves as Level 1 of the Dense resolution tower, with monotone spectrum growth 7 → 8 from Dense-50. It is also the target for the thermodynamic integration (`CoarseGrainedRT` + `HalfBoundWitness` in `Bridge/Dense100Thermodynamics.agda`) and the coarse-graining witness (`Bridge/CoarseGrain.agda`).

## 2. Topology

The Dense-100 patch is grown by greedy max-connectivity from the central cube in the {4,3,5} hyperbolic honeycomb. At each step, the frontier cell with the most existing neighbors in the patch is added. This produces a compact, densely-connected cluster — unlike the BFS-shell strategy that grows in concentric shells and produces thin, uniformly-connected boundaries.

**Growth strategy comparison (from `07_honeycomb_3d_multiStrategy_OUTPUT.txt`):**

| Property | BFS-shell (108 cells) | Dense (100 cells) |
|----------|----------------------|--------------------|
| Growth rule | Add all cells at BFS distance ≤ d | Add frontier cell with max adjacency |
| Density (2·IntF/Cells) | 2.67 | **3.00** |
| Min-cut range | 1–2 | **1–8** |
| Boundary structure | Thin shell, ~1 connection to interior | Multiply-connected, 1–5+ connections |
| RT surface depth | Trivial (single-face cuts) | **Genuine multi-face separating surfaces** |

The Dense strategy is the 3D analogue of "packing pentagons" in the {5,4} tiling: local density drives holographic depth. Boundary regions require cutting through up to 8 internal cube-faces to disconnect them — a genuine 2D separating surface in the 3D bulk.

## 4. The Orbit Reduction Strategy

The Dense-100 patch is the **first instance in the repository to use orbit reduction**. The 717 regions share only 8 distinct min-cut values. The observable functions `S-cut` and `L-min` are defined on the small orbit type `D100OrbitRep` (8 constructors), then composed with a classification function `classify100 : D100Region → D100OrbitRep` (717 clauses). The proof obligations reduce from 717 pointwise `refl` cases to 8 orbit-representative `refl` cases + a 1-line lifting.

**Orbit decomposition:**

| Orbit | Min-cut | Regions | Agda constructor |
|-------|---------|---------|-----------------|
| mc1 | 1 | 4 | `mc1` |
| mc2 | 2 | 72 | `mc2` |
| mc3 | 3 | 62 | `mc3` |
| mc4 | 4 | 59 | `mc4` |
| mc5 | 5 | 137 | `mc5` |
| mc6 | 6 | 146 | `mc6` |
| mc7 | 7 | 227 | `mc7` |
| mc8 | 8 | 10 | `mc8` |

**Reduction factor:** 717 → 8 (90× fewer proof cases).

**The 1-line lifting:**

```agda
d100-pointwise r = d100-pointwise-rep (classify100 r)
```

This works because both `S-cut` and `L-min` are *defined* as their orbit-representative versions composed with the classification function:

```agda
S-cut _ r = S-cut-rep (classify100 r)
L-min _ r = L-min-rep (classify100 r)
```

The 717-clause classification function is traversed only during concrete evaluation; the proof obligations remain on the 8-constructor orbit type. See [`engineering/orbit-reduction.md`](../engineering/orbit-reduction.md) for the full scaling analysis.

**Comparison with Dense-50 (flat enumeration):**

| Pointwise proof | 139 `refl` cases | 8 `refl` cases + 1-line lifting |
| Proof size | O(N) where N = regions | O(K) where K = orbits |

## 6. Three Levels of Entropy-Area Constraint

The Dense-100 patch carries **three progressively stronger constraints** on the entropy-area relationship:

| Level | Statement | Module | Constraint |
|-------|-----------|--------|------------|
| 1 (RT) | S(A) = L(A) | `Bridge/GenericBridge.agda` via `Bridge/Dense100Obs.agda` | Exact correspondence |
| 2 (Area law) | S(A) ≤ area(A) | `Boundary/Dense100AreaLaw.agda` | Isoperimetric inequality |
| 3 (Half-bound) | 2·S(A) ≤ area(A) | `Boundary/Dense100HalfBound.agda` | **Sharp Bekenstein–Hawking** |

Level 3 subsumes Level 2 (since S ≤ 2·S ≤ area for non-negative S). Level 1 is independent (it is an equality, not a bound). All three cohere through the same observable `S-cut d100BdyView` and the same area function `regionArea`.

### 6.2 The Sharp Half-Bound (Level 3)

The half-bound 2·S(A) ≤ area(A) is verified on all 717 regions in `Boundary/Dense100HalfBound.agda` (1390 lines, auto-generated by `17_generate_half_bound.py`, sealed behind `abstract`).

**Half-bound statistics (from `17_generate_half_bound_OUTPUT.txt`):**

The 40 achiever regions demonstrate that the bound is **tight**: there exist regions where 2·S(A) = area(A), identifying the discrete Newton's constant as 1/(4G) = 1/2 in bond-dimension-1 units.

**Achiever mechanism:** A single cube with exactly 3 neighbors in the patch has n_cross = 3 (crossing bonds) and n_bdy = 3 (boundary legs), giving area = 6 and S = min(3, 3) = 3. Since 2·3 = 6 = area, the bound is saturated. This is the discrete analogue of a cell where exactly half its faces cross to neighbors and half are exposed as boundary legs.

## 16. Historical Significance

The Dense-100 patch is the repository's **architectural pivot** — the point where the orbit reduction strategy was validated and the three-level entropy-area constraint architecture was established.

### 16.1 The Orbit Reduction Innovation

Before Dense-100, every bridge instance used flat enumeration: one `refl` per region constructor. The Dense-50 patch (139 regions, 139 flat `refl` proofs) was at the practical limit of this approach. Dense-100 (717 regions) would have required 717 flat `refl` proofs — wasteful when all 717 share only 8 distinct min-cut values.

The orbit reduction strategy (`OrbitReducedPatch` in `Bridge/GenericBridge.agda`) was designed specifically for this scaling challenge:

> **Original:** 717 flat `refl` proofs (one per region)
> **New:** 8 orbit `refl` proofs + 1-line lifting (via `classify100`)

The 1-line lifting `d100-pointwise r = d100-pointwise-rep (classify100 r)` became the template for all subsequent orbit-reduced instances: Dense-200 (1246 → 9), {5,4} depth-2 through depth-7 (15–1885 → 2).

### 16.2 The Entropic Convergence Resolution

The Dense-100 patch was central to the discovery of the sharp half-bound S ≤ area/2. The Python oracle scripts 14a–14c tracked the ratio η_N = max S/max area across resolutions, discovering that the pointwise supremum sup(S/area) = 1/2 exactly — independent of patch size, growth strategy, tiling type, and bond capacity. Script 15 proved this follows from the two-cut decomposition area = n_cross + n_bdy. Script 17 generated the per-instance `HalfBoundWitness` modules.

The half-bound **eliminates the constructive-reals wall** for the entropy-area relationship: the discrete Newton's constant 1/(4G) = 1/2 is an exact rational verified by `refl` on closed ℕ terms, not a real-valued limit.

### 16.3 The Resolution Tower Foundation

The Dense-100 establishes the middle level of the 3-level convergence certificate:

- Dense-50 (maxS = 7) — the baseline
- **Dense-100 (maxS = 8)** — monotonicity witness `(1, refl)` for 7 ≤ 8
- Dense-200 (maxS = 9) — monotonicity witness `(1, refl)` for 8 ≤ 9

The `ConvergenceCertificate3L-HB` type in `Bridge/SchematicTower.agda` carries all three levels with monotonicity witnesses and sharp half-bounds, culminating in the `DiscreteBekensteinHawking` capstone type alias.
