# Dense-200 Instance

**The 200-cell {4,3,5} Dense patch — the third resolution tower level, carrying three levels of entropy-area constraint with monotone spectrum growth 8 → 9.**

**Modules:** `Common/Dense200Spec.agda` (AUTO-GEN), `Boundary/Dense200Cut.agda` (AUTO-GEN), `Bulk/Dense200Chain.agda` (AUTO-GEN), `Bulk/Dense200Curvature.agda` (AUTO-GEN), `Bridge/Dense200Obs.agda` (AUTO-GEN), `Boundary/Dense200AreaLaw.agda` (AUTO-GEN, `abstract`), `Boundary/Dense200HalfBound.agda` (AUTO-GEN, `abstract`)

**Role:** The Dense-200 patch extends the resolution tower from two levels (Dense-50, Dense-100) to three levels (Dense-50, Dense-100, Dense-200), confirming monotone growth of the min-cut spectrum: 7 → 8 → 9. It carries the same **three levels of entropy-area constraint** as Dense-100 — the RT correspondence (S = L), the discrete area law (S ≤ area), and the sharp Bekenstein–Hawking half-bound (2·S ≤ area with tight achiever) — and demonstrates that the orbit reduction architecture scales smoothly from 717 regions / 8 orbits (Dense-100) to 1246 regions / 9 orbits (Dense-200). Together with Dense-50 and Dense-100, the Dense-200 level populates the `ConvergenceCertificate3L-HB` and the capstone `DiscreteBekensteinHawking` type in `Bridge/SchematicTower.agda`.

## 2. Topology

The Dense-200 patch is grown by greedy max-connectivity from the central cube in the {4,3,5} hyperbolic honeycomb. At each step, the frontier cell with the most existing neighbors in the patch is added. This produces a compact, densely-connected cluster — the natural 3D extension of "packing pentagons" in the {5,4} tiling.

**Growth strategy comparison (from `07_honeycomb_3d_multiStrategy_OUTPUT.txt`):**

| Min-cut range | 1–2 | **1–9** |
| Boundary structure | Thin shell, ~1 connection to interior | Multiply-connected, 1–5+ connections |

All 12 edges of the central cube are fully surrounded by 5 cubes from the patch, meeting the full {4,3,5} Schläfli condition. This confirms genuine 3D hyperbolic geometry: the angular excess at each fully-surrounded edge is 5 × 90° − 360° = 90°, giving a negative edge deficit κ = −π/2.

## 4. The Orbit Reduction Strategy

The Dense-200 patch uses the same **orbit reduction architecture** as Dense-100. The 1246 regions share only 9 distinct min-cut values. The observable functions `S-cut` and `L-min` are defined on the small orbit type `D200OrbitRep` (9 constructors), then composed with a classification function `classify200 : D200Region → D200OrbitRep` (1246 clauses). The proof obligations reduce from 1246 pointwise `refl` cases to 9 orbit-representative `refl` cases + a 1-line lifting.

**Orbit decomposition:**

| Orbit | Min-cut | Regions | Agda constructor |
|-------|---------|---------|-----------------|
| mc1 | 1 | 6 | `mc1` |
| mc2 | 2 | 135 | `mc2` |
| mc3 | 3 | 80 | `mc3` |
| mc4 | 4 | 119 | `mc4` |
| mc5 | 5 | 172 | `mc5` |
| mc6 | 6 | 258 | `mc6` |
| mc7 | 7 | 366 | `mc7` |
| mc8 | 8 | 74 | `mc8` |
| mc9 | 9 | 36 | `mc9` |

**Reduction factor:** 1246 → 9 (138× fewer proof cases).

**The 1-line lifting:**

```agda
d200-pointwise r = d200-pointwise-rep (classify200 r)
```

This works because both `S-cut` and `L-min` are *defined* as their orbit-representative versions composed with the classification function:

```agda
S-cut _ r = S-cut-rep (classify200 r)
L-min _ r = L-min-rep (classify200 r)
```

**Comparison of orbit reduction across the tower:**

| Patch | Regions | Orbits | Reduction | Proof cases |
|-------|---------|--------|-----------|-------------|
| Dense-50 | 139 | — | flat | 139 |
| Dense-100 | 717 | 8 | 90× | 8 |
| **Dense-200** | **1246** | **9** | **138×** | **9** |

Adding 100 cells grew the classification function from 717 to 1246 clauses, but the proof obligations grew only from 8 to 9 orbit `refl` cases. This validates the orbit reduction scaling claim: adding tiles grows the `classify` function only, not the proof obligations.

## 6. Three Levels of Entropy-Area Constraint

The Dense-200 patch carries **three progressively stronger constraints** on the entropy-area relationship — the same architecture as Dense-100:

| Level | Statement | Module | Constraint |
|-------|-----------|--------|------------|
| 1 (RT) | S(A) = L(A) | `Bridge/GenericBridge.agda` via `Bridge/Dense200Obs.agda` | Exact correspondence |
| 2 (Area law) | S(A) ≤ area(A) | `Boundary/Dense200AreaLaw.agda` | Isoperimetric inequality |
| 3 (Half-bound) | 2·S(A) ≤ area(A) | `Boundary/Dense200HalfBound.agda` | **Sharp Bekenstein–Hawking** |

### 6.1 The Discrete Area Law (Level 2)

The area law S(A) ≤ area(A) is verified on all 1246 regions in `Boundary/Dense200AreaLaw.agda` (3556 lines, auto-generated by `12_generate_dense200.py`, sealed behind `abstract`).

| Slack (area − S) range | 3–18 |

| Tight cases (S = area) | 0 (the area law is never tight) |

### 6.2 The Sharp Half-Bound (Level 3)

The half-bound 2·S(A) ≤ area(A) is verified on all 1246 regions in `Boundary/Dense200HalfBound.agda` (2350 lines, auto-generated by `17_generate_half_bound.py`, sealed behind `abstract`).

**Half-bound statistics (from `17_generate_half_bound_OUTPUT.txt`):**

The 88 achiever regions demonstrate that the bound is **tight**: there exist regions where 2·S(A) = area(A), identifying the discrete Newton's constant as 1/(4G) = 1/2 in bond-dimension-1 units.

**Achiever breakdown:**

| Region size (k) | Achiever count | Example |

The achievers at k = 2 and k = 3 demonstrate that equality is not restricted to single-cell regions: 2-cell regions with S = 5, area = 10 and 3-cell regions with S = 7, area = 14 also saturate the bound. Compare with Dense-100, which had only k = 1 achievers (40 regions with S = 3, area = 6).

## 7. 3D Edge Curvature

In 3 dimensions, the combinatorial curvature lives on **edges** (not vertices). At an interior edge e with valence v:

> κ(e) = 2π − v · (π/2)

For the Dense-200 patch, all 12 central-cube edges are fully surrounded by 5 cubes from the patch:

| Edge class | Valence | Count | κ₂₀ (twentieths) | Interpretation |
|------------|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| ev5 | 5 | 12 | −5 | Hyperbolic (full {4,3,5} condition) |

**Total edge curvature:**


## 13. What the Dense-200 Patch Does NOT Exercise

- **Flat enumeration.** The Dense-200 uses orbit reduction exclusively. Flat enumeration (one `refl` per region constructor) was used by Dense-50 ([`instances/dense-50.md`](dense-50.md)) and earlier patches.

- **Subadditivity / monotonicity structural properties.** These are exercised on the 6-tile star patch ([`instances/star-patch.md`](star-patch.md)) and the 11-tile filled patch ([`instances/filled-patch.md`](filled-patch.md)).

- **Parameterized step invariance / dynamics.** The dynamics layer operates on the 6-tile star with parameterized weights. See [`instances/star-patch.md`](star-patch.md).

- **Gauge connections / quantum superposition.** The gauge and quantum layers are exercised on the 6-tile star. See [`instances/star-patch.md`](star-patch.md).

- **Curvature-agnostic bridge (Wick rotation).** The Wick rotation is demonstrated between AdS ({5,4}) and dS ({5,3}) on the star patch. See [`instances/desitter-patch.md`](desitter-patch.md).

- **2D polygon-complex / vertex curvature.** The Dense-200 is 3D with edge curvature; the 2D vertex curvature and Gauss–Bonnet theorem are exercised by the filled patch ([`instances/filled-patch.md`](filled-patch.md)).

## 15. Historical Significance

The Dense-200 patch extends the resolution tower from two levels to three, providing the **empirical confirmation of monotone spectrum growth** that was the "key empirical question" of §10.5 of the historical development docs:

> "Does the max min-cut grow to 9 or beyond?"
>
> Dense-50 max = 7, Dense-100 max = 8 → Dense-200 max = **9**  ✓

The answer — yes, the spectrum grows to 9 — confirms the monotone convergence pattern and justifies the `ConvergenceCertificate3L-HB` type.

### 15.1 The Half-Bound at Scale

The Dense-200 half-bound is the first instance with **multi-size achievers**: regions of 1, 2, and 3 cells all saturate the bound 2·S = area. This demonstrates that the discrete Newton's constant 1/(4G) = 1/2 is not just a small-region phenomenon — it persists at larger region sizes where the min-cut involves cutting through multiple internal faces.

**Comparison of achievers across the tower:**

| Patch | Achiever count | Achiever sizes |
|-------|---------------|----------------|
| Dense-100 | 40 | k = 1 only (S = 3, area = 6) |
| Dense-200 | 88 | k = 1 (80), k = 2 (4), k = 3 (4) |

### 15.2 The Constructive-Reals Bypass

The Dense-200 level contributes to the `ConvergenceCertificate3L-HB` which **replaces** the `ConvergenceWitness` from §15.9.5 of the historical development docs. The original `ConvergenceWitness` would have required constructive reals and Cauchy completeness to formalize the limit of η_N. The sharp half-bound eliminates this: the discrete Newton's constant 1/(4G) = 1/2 is an exact rational verified by `refl` on closed ℕ terms at every resolution level — no limit argument needed.
