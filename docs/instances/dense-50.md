# Dense-50 Instance

**The 50-cell {4,3,5} Dense patch — the first 3D instance with genuine holographic depth (min-cut range 1–7).**

**Modules:** `Common/Dense50Spec.agda` (AUTO-GEN), `Boundary/Dense50Cut.agda` (AUTO-GEN), `Bulk/Dense50Chain.agda` (AUTO-GEN), `Bulk/Dense50Curvature.agda` (AUTO-GEN), `Bridge/Dense50Obs.agda` (AUTO-GEN), `Bridge/Dense50Equiv.agda` (DEAD CODE)

**Role:** The Dense-50 patch is the first 3D instance grown by greedy max-connectivity (the Dense strategy) rather than BFS concentric shells. It demonstrates that the Dense growth strategy produces multiply-connected bulk geometry with non-trivial min-cut values (2–7), confirming genuine multi-face Ryu–Takayanagi separating surfaces in the 3D bulk — unlike the BFS-shell honeycomb patch ([`instances/honeycomb-3d.md`](honeycomb-3d.md)) where all singleton min-cuts are 1. It is the last instance to use flat enumeration (no orbit reduction); the Dense-100 patch ([`instances/dense-100.md`](dense-100.md)) introduces orbit reduction to handle the 717-region scale.

## 2. Topology

The Dense-50 patch is grown by greedy max-connectivity from the central cube in the {4,3,5} hyperbolic honeycomb. At each step, the frontier cell with the most existing neighbors in the patch is added. This produces a compact, densely-connected cluster — unlike the BFS-shell strategy that grows in concentric shells and produces thin, uniformly-connected boundaries.

**Growth strategy comparison:**

| Property | BFS-shell (Honeycomb) | Dense (this patch) |
|----------|----------------------|--------------------|
| Growth rule | Add all cells at BFS distance ≤ d | Add frontier cell with max adjacency |
| Density (2·IntF/Cells) | 2.44 (at 32 cells) | 2.72 (at 50 cells) |
| Min-cut range | 1 (all singletons) | 1–7 |
| Boundary structure | Thin shell, ~1 connection to interior | Multiply-connected, 1–3+ connections |
| RT surface depth | Trivial (single-face cuts) | Genuine multi-face separating surfaces |

The Dense strategy is the 3D analogue of "packing pentagons" in the {5,4} tiling: local density drives holographic depth. The BFS strategy produces concentric shells where every boundary cell connects to the interior through approximately one face, giving min-cut ≈ 1 for all singletons. The Dense strategy produces a bulk where some boundary cells are deeply embedded, requiring cutting through 5, 6, or 7 internal faces to disconnect them.

**Patch structure:**

| Count | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Cells (cubes) | 50 |
| Internal shared faces | 68 |
| Boundary faces | 164 |
| Faces per cell | 6 |
| All 12 central-cube edges at full valence 5 | ✓ |

All 12 edges of the central cube are fully surrounded by 5 cubes from the patch, meeting the full {4,3,5} Schläfli condition. This confirms genuine 3D hyperbolic geometry: the angular excess at each fully-surrounded edge is 5 × 90° − 360° = 90°, giving a negative edge deficit κ = −π/2.

## 4. Min-Cut / Observable Agreement

All 139 pointwise agreement cases hold by `refl` because both `S-cut` and `L-min` return the same ℕ literals. The `refl` proofs depend on both sides reducing to identical normal forms — guaranteed by the shared-constants discipline (constants defined once in `Util/Scalars.agda`).

**Representative sample:**

| Region | Cells | S_cut | L_min | S = L? |
|--------|-------|-------|-------|--------|
| d50r0 | 1 cell | 1 | 1 | ✓ `refl` |
| d50r1 | 2 cells | 3 | 3 | ✓ `refl` |
| d50r4 | 5 cells | 7 | 7 | ✓ `refl` |
| d50r15 | 5 cells | 8 | — | (not in Dense-50; max S = 7) |

**Physical interpretation:** In 3D, the min-cut is a set of internal *faces* (a minimal separating surface), not edges. A min-cut value S = 7 means that severing 7 shared cube-faces is needed to disconnect the boundary region from its complement — a genuine 2D surface cutting through the 3D bulk. This is the 3D discrete Ryu–Takayanagi correspondence: boundary entanglement entropy equals bulk minimal surface area.

## 5. 3D Edge Curvature

In 3 dimensions, the combinatorial curvature lives on **edges** (not vertices). At an interior edge e of a polyhedral 3-complex with valence v:

> κ(e) = 2π − v · (dihedral angle)

For the {4,3,5} honeycomb (5 cubes per edge, dihedral angle π/2):

> κ(e) = 2π − 5 · π/2 = −π/2

In the Dense-50 patch, all 12 central-cube edges are fully surrounded by 5 cubes from the patch:

| Edge class | Valence | Count | κ₂₀ (twentieths) | Interpretation |
|------------|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| ev5 | 5 | 12 | −5 | Hyperbolic (full {4,3,5} condition) |

**Total edge curvature:**

```
12 · (−5) = −60  (in twentieths)
```

Verified by `refl` in `Bulk/Dense50Curvature.agda`:

```agda
totalD50Curvature-check : totalD50Curvature ≡ negsuc 59
totalD50Curvature-check = refl
```

The total is `negsuc 59` because −60 in ℤ is `negsuc 59` (representing −(59+1) = −60).

## 10. What the Dense-50 Patch Does NOT Exercise

- **Orbit reduction.** The Dense-50 patch has 139 regions with 7 distinct min-cut values — orbit reduction would reduce proof cases from 139 to 7. However, 139 regions are still manageable with flat enumeration, so orbit reduction is deferred to Dense-100 ([`instances/dense-100.md`](dense-100.md)) where the 717-region count makes it necessary.

- **`abstract` barrier.** The 139-case pointwise proof is small enough to normalize quickly without sealing. The `abstract` pattern is first used for the area law at Dense-100 (`Boundary/Dense100AreaLaw.agda` with 717 `abstract` cases).

- **Area law / Bekenstein–Hawking bound.** The discrete area law S ≤ area and the half-bound 2·S ≤ area are first verified on Dense-100 ([`instances/dense-100.md`](dense-100.md)) and Dense-200 ([`instances/dense-200.md`](dense-200.md)).

- **Resolution tower.** The Dense-50 patch serves as the first level (maxCut = 7) of the Dense resolution tower, but the tower infrastructure (`Bridge/SchematicTower.agda`) treats it as a baseline — the monotonicity witnesses track the growth Dense-50 (7) → Dense-100 (8) → Dense-200 (9).

- **Parameterized step invariance / dynamics.** The dynamics layer operates on the 6-tile star with parameterized weights. See [`instances/star-patch.md`](star-patch.md).

- **Gauge connections / quantum superposition.** The gauge and quantum layers are exercised on the 6-tile star. See [`instances/star-patch.md`](star-patch.md).

- **Curvature-agnostic bridge (Wick rotation).** The Wick rotation is demonstrated between AdS ({5,4}) and dS ({5,3}) on the star patch. See [`instances/desitter-patch.md`](desitter-patch.md).

## 12. Historical Significance

The Dense-50 patch is the repository's **first genuinely deep 3D holographic instance** — the first patch where boundary regions require cutting through multi-face separating surfaces (up to 7 faces) to disconnect them from their complement. This is the 3D analogue of the N-singleton discrepancy in 2D: the BFS-shell patches produce "thin" boundaries where min-cut ≈ 1, while the Dense strategy produces "thick" boundaries where the holographic correspondence involves genuinely non-trivial bulk geometry.

The Dense-50 patch was developed as part of the multi-strategy comparison in `07_honeycomb_3d_multiStrategy.py` (§6 of the historical development docs), which identified the Dense growth strategy as the correct approach for holographic formalization:

> "Dense growth is the 3D analog of 'packing pentagons' in the 2D {5,4} tiling — local density drives holographic depth."

The key finding from the multi-strategy comparison:

| Strategy | Max min-cut at 50 cells | Density |
|----------|------------------------|---------|
| BFS-shell | 1 | 2.44 |
| **Dense** | **7** | **2.72** |
| Geodesic | 2 | 1.96 |
| Hemisphere | (saturates at 20 cells) | 3.00 |

The Dense strategy produces 7× deeper holographic surfaces than BFS at the same cell count, confirming that multiply-connected bulk geometry is essential for non-trivial RT surfaces.

## 13. The Dense Growth Strategy

The Dense strategy grows the patch by greedy max-connectivity: at each step, the frontier cell with the most neighbors already in the patch is added. This produces a compact, roughly spherical cluster where:

1. **Interior cells** are deeply embedded (all 6 faces shared → 0 boundary legs).
2. **Boundary cells** have varying connectivity (1–5 shared faces → 1–5 boundary legs).
3. **High-connectivity boundary cells** require cutting through many internal faces to disconnect, giving high min-cut values.

The density metric `2·IntF/Cells` measures how many internal face-pairs exist per cell. Higher density means more multiply-connected bulk:

| Patch | Cells | Density | Max S |
|-------|-------|---------|-------|
| BFS-shell (32 cells) | 32 | 2.44 | 1 |
| **Dense-50** | **50** | **2.72** | **7** |
| Dense-100 | 100 | 3.00 | 8 |
| Dense-200 | 200 | 3.08 | 9 |

The density grows monotonically with cell count for the Dense strategy, reflecting the hyperbolic geometry: in a hyperbolic ball, the ratio of volume to surface area increases with radius. This is the discrete analogue of the UV/IR connection in AdS/CFT.

## 15. The Resolution Tower Role

The Dense-50 patch serves as the first level (Level 0) of the Dense resolution tower in `Bridge/SchematicTower.agda`:

| Level | Patch | Cells | Regions | Orbits | Max S | Monotone |
|-------|-------|-------|---------|--------|-------|----------|
| **0** | **Dense-50** | **50** | **139** | **—** | **7** | **—** |
| 1 | Dense-100 | 100 | 717 | 8 | 8 | (1, refl) |
| 2 | Dense-200 | 200 | 1246 | 9 | 9 | (1, refl) |

The min-cut spectrum grows monotonically: 7 → 8 → 9. The Dense-50 level provides the baseline for the first monotonicity witness `spectrum-grows-50-100 : 7 ≤ℚ 8` (= `(1 , refl)` because `1 + 7 = 8` judgmentally).

Although the Dense-50 patch does not use orbit reduction, its role in the tower is well-defined: it is the coarsest resolution level, and its max min-cut value (7) establishes the lower bound for the monotone spectrum growth.
