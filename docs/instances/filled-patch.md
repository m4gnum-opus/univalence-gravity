## 4. The N-Singleton Discrepancy and Its Resolution

Phase 1.2 prototyping (`01_happy_patch_cuts.py`) revealed that for N-type singleton regions on the 11-tile patch, the boundary min-cut is 2 while the internal-only geodesic is 3. The min-cut routes through the 2 boundary legs of the N-tile rather than severing the 3 internal bonds. For all regions of size ‚â• 2 and for all G-type singletons, S = geodesic.

**Resolution (¬ß3.2 of the historical development docs):** The bulk observable functional is redefined to represent the **true min-cut** (allowing it to sever boundary bonds, not just internal ones). Under this definition, the boundary min-cut and the bulk minimal separating chain agree on all 90 contiguous tile-aligned regions. Both `S-cut` and `L-min` in the generated modules return the same ‚Ñï literal for each region, and all 90 pointwise agreement proofs hold by `refl`.

This resolution is why the filled patch's observable functions in `Boundary/FilledCut.agda` and `Bulk/FilledChain.agda` both include boundary-leg contributions in the min-cut computation, unlike the star patch (where boundary legs are never part of the optimal cut).

## 7. Subadditivity (360 Cases, `abstract` Barrier)

The boundary min-cut functional is subadditive: S(A‚à™B) ‚â§ S(A) + S(B) for all 360 representable union triples on the 90-region type. The proof is generated by `03_generate_filled_patch.py` and sealed behind `abstract` in `Boundary/FilledSubadditivity.agda`.

**Union triple naming:** `u‚ü®startA‚ü©-‚ü®lenA‚ü©-‚ü®lenB‚ü©` where A starts at group `startA` with `lenA` groups, B immediately follows with `lenB` groups.

**Subadditivity witness distribution (360 triples):**

| Witness (k) | Count | Inequality |
|-------------|-------|------------|
| (1, refl) | 30 | S(union) = S(A) + S(B) ‚àí 1 |
| (2, refl) | 100 | S(union) = S(A) + S(B) ‚àí 2 |
| (3, refl) | 100 | S(union) = S(A) + S(B) ‚àí 3 |
| (4, refl) | 50 | S(union) = S(A) + S(B) ‚àí 4 |
| (5, refl) | 50 | S(union) = S(A) + S(B) ‚àí 5 |
| (6, refl) | 30 | S(union) = S(A) + S(B) ‚àí 6 |

The `abstract` barrier prevents downstream modules (e.g., `Bridge/FilledEquiv.agda`) from re-normalizing the 360 `(k, refl)` proof trees when they use the subadditivity lemma. Since `_‚â§‚Ñö_` is propositional (by `isProp‚â§‚Ñö`), the barrier does not damage any Univalence bridge or transport computation. This is the first use of the `abstract` barrier pattern in the repository, documented in [`engineering/abstract-barrier.md`](../engineering/abstract-barrier.md) and [Agda Issue #4573](https://github.com/agda/agda/issues/4573).

### 8.3 Enriched Equivalence (FilledEquiv.agda)

The hand-written `Bridge/FilledEquiv.agda` assembles the enriched type equivalence from the auto-generated components. Its architecture mirrors `Bridge/EnrichedStarObs.agda` from the 6-tile star, scaled to the 90-region / 360-union setting:

- **Enriched types:** `FilledEnrichedBdy = Œ£[ f ‚àà (FilledRegion ‚Üí ‚Ñö‚â•0) ] (f ‚â° S‚àÇF)` and `FilledEnrichedBulk = Œ£[ f ‚àà (FilledRegion ‚Üí ‚Ñö‚â•0) ] (f ‚â° LBF)` ‚Äî reversed singleton types, genuinely different in the universe.
- **Iso construction:** Forward map appends `filled-obs-path`; inverse appends `sym filled-obs-path`. Round-trip proofs close by `isSet` of the function space.
- **ua + transport:** `isoToEquiv` ‚Üí `ua` ‚Üí `uaŒ≤` ‚Üí verified transport.

### 8.4 Raw Structural Equivalence (FilledRawEquiv.agda)

The auto-generated `Bridge/FilledRawEquiv.agda` (from `04_generate_raw_equiv.py`) constructs a raw type-level equivalence between the 15-dimensional bulk bond-weight space and the 90-dimensional boundary min-cut space:

- **Forward map (RT direction):** 90-clause `minCutFromWeights` expressing each min-cut as a sum of bond weights or a constant.
- **Backward map (peeling strategy):** 15-clause `weightsFromMinCut` recovering bond weights via G-singleton extraction + truncated subtraction.
- **RT lemma:** 90 `refl` proofs (forward map at canonical weights = S‚àÇF).
- **Extraction lemma:** 15 `refl` proofs (backward map on S‚àÇF = filledWeight).
- Both raw types are contractible ‚Üí round-trip proofs close by `isContr‚ÜíisProp`.

## 14. Historical Significance

The filled patch is the repository's **first curvature test object** and the **first scaled bridge target**. It was described in ¬ß2.2 and ¬ß5.1 of `historical/development-docs/09-happy-instance.md` as the natural next step after the 6-tile star:

> "The 11-tile filled patch is the primary curvature / Gauss‚ÄìBonnet formalization target for Theorem 1. It is the smallest {5,4} patch that forms a proper disk (no gaps), has interior vertices with full valence 4, exhibits negative curvature, and supports Gauss‚ÄìBonnet with both interior and boundary terms."

The filled patch was the first module to encounter the **AST/RAM wall** documented in ¬ß3.4 of `historical/development-docs/10-frontier.md`: attempting to type-check 360 subadditivity cases in a single Agda module exhausted available RAM. The solution ‚Äî the **combined Approach A+C strategy** (Python oracle generates `(k, refl)` witnesses; `abstract` seals them) ‚Äî became the standard proof engineering pattern for all subsequent large-patch modules. This is documented in detail in [`engineering/abstract-barrier.md`](../engineering/abstract-barrier.md).

The filled patch also led to the discovery of the **N-singleton discrepancy** (¬ß3.2 of the historical development docs): the boundary min-cut for single N-tiles uses boundary legs rather than internal bonds, giving S = 2 while the internal-only geodesic gives 3. This concrete finding validated the roadmap's recommendation to prototype numerically before formalizing, and informed the design choice to define the bulk observable as the full min-cut (including boundary bonds).
